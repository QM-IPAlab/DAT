Abstract

We address a critical bottleneck in cooperative open-vocabulary object detection: the Global–Local Feature Imbalance in pre-trained Vision-Language Models (VLMs). While VLMs excel at whole-image understanding, they struggle with cropped region proposals during detection, leading to suboptimal performance on novel object categories. To bridge this gap, we introduce Decoupled Adaptivity Training (DAT), a self-supervised fine-tuning paradigm that efficiently adapts VLMs to region-level recognition without compromising their zero-shot capabilities.

DAT operates exclusively on the visual backbone using in-pipeline pseudo-labels from a frozen detector, constructing a perfectly domain-aligned training set. Through a novel combination of a sigmoid-based contrastive loss for robust multi-label learning and WiSE-FT for global knowledge preservation, DAT achieves significant improvements in novel-class detection while maintaining strong base-class performance—all with minimal computational overhead. Our method establishes new state-of-the-art results on COCO and LVIS benchmarks, providing a simple yet effective pathway toward more generalizable open-vocabulary vision systems.

Methodology

Decoupled Adaptivity Training (DAT) consists of two key stages:

1. Region-Aware Data Construction

We construct a domain-aligned dataset $\mathcal{D}_{\text{region}}$ by cropping object proposals using a pre-trained Mask R-CNN. With hyperparameters $N_{\text{max}}=80$ (max proposals per image) and $\tau_{\text{conf}}=0.7$ (confidence threshold), we generate approximately 400K high-quality region-text pairs from 5K source images.

2. Decoupled Fine-Tuning

We fine-tune only the visual encoder of the VLM on $\mathcal{D}_{\text{region}}$ using a region-level contrastive objective. To preserve zero-shot capabilities, we employ WiSE-FT (Weight-Space Ensembling for Fine-Tuning) with interpolation coefficient $\alpha=0.5$. This ensures the adapted model $\mathcal{V}^{*}$ maintains its global semantic understanding while enhancing region-level discriminability.

Results

Main Results on COCO and LVIS

DAT achieves 70.1 AP50 on novel categories in COCO 48/17 split, significantly outperforming all existing cooperative and end-to-end methods. On LVIS, DAT improves novel category AP by +0.26 over the strong CFM baseline while maintaining excellent known category performance.

Key Findings

  • Efficiency: DAT fine-tuning completes in under 2 hours on a single GPU.
  • Effectiveness: 20+ percentage point improvement over vanilla cooperative baselines.
  • Generality: Consistent gains across both COCO and LVIS benchmarks.

Ablation Studies

We conduct comprehensive ablations to validate DAT's design choices:

Dataset Scale

Performance peaks at 500-1,000 images (~40K-80K crops), confirming that sufficient but not excessive data yields optimal adaptation.

WiSE-FT Interpolation

WiSE-FT ($\alpha=0.5$) improves LVIS novel AP by +1.7 with minimal COCO impact, demonstrating effective knowledge preservation.
