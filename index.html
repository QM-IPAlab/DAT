1Queen Mary University of London

Abstract

We address a critical bottleneck in cooperative open-vocabulary object detection: the Global–Local Feature Imbalance in pre-trained Vision-Language Models (VLMs). While VLMs excel at whole-image understanding, they struggle with cropped region proposals during detection, leading to suboptimal performance on novel object categories.

To bridge this gap, we introduce Decoupled Adaptivity Training (DAT), a self-supervised fine-tuning paradigm that efficiently adapts VLMs to region-level recognition without compromising their zero-shot capabilities. DAT operates exclusively on the visual backbone using in-pipeline pseudo-labels from a frozen detector, constructing a perfectly domain-aligned training set.

Through a novel combination of a sigmoid-based contrastive loss for robust multi-label learning and WiSE-FT for global knowledge preservation, DAT achieves significant improvements in novel-class detection while maintaining strong base-class performance—all with minimal computational overhead. Our method establishes new state-of-the-art results on COCO and LVIS benchmarks.

Methodology

Decoupled Adaptivity Training (DAT) consists of two key stages:

  • Region-Aware Data Construction: We construct a domain-aligned dataset by cropping object proposals using a pre-trained Mask R-CNN. With hyperparameters \(N_{\text{max}}=80\) and \(\tau_{\text{conf}}=0.7\), we generate high-quality region-text pairs.
  • Decoupled Fine-Tuning: We fine-tune only the visual encoder of the VLM on the region dataset using a region-level contrastive objective. To preserve zero-shot capabilities, we employ WiSE-FT with interpolation coefficient \(\alpha=0.5\).

Results

DAT achieves 70.1 AP50 on novel categories in COCO 48/17 split, significantly outperforming all existing cooperative and end-to-end methods. On LVIS, DAT improves novel category AP by +0.26 over the strong CFM baseline while maintaining excellent known category performance.
